The Easiest Coffee Tookit Worth Wanting and How to Ratchet Up

First off, before anyone says anything about this topic- there's already a once sentence answer.


Cold-brewed concentrate, which you can usually purchase from a good grocery store by the milk. Some taste like medicine so watch out but that's one thing to explore if you don't have any time.

Anyways, hopefully you are leaving yourself time in the morning to relax

(i.e. getting up near the time you set your alarm to go off), because that is one great way to start your day off on the right foot.

The basics are a grinder, teapot - or watter heater, and a french press ##leave this side by side

One could argue there are other ways to do this cheaper:

  • Is the grinder an option...clearly no
  • Is the teapot or water heater and option...NO
  • Ding ding ding...you can replace the fp...and there are a million and one ways....Aero, chemex or other pourover, bodum coffee, italian stovetop bialetti...


But this is the way I chose because there's both 1 the least amount of effortful cleanup, and 2 you can get fancy with it

(Clearly I'm talking about my ultra sheek coffee station lol)

  1. Whole beans are an obvious choice because....just smell freshly ground (preferably of a certain caliber quality) beans and then go sniff some folgers. Do that experiment before you argue with me on this one, honey.
  2. NEXT grind them...read instructions on grinder...
  3. Pour grounds into FP
  4. Pour hot, but not boiling!! water into FP all at once
  5. If you're fancy let sit for ~5 mins
  6. If you're in a hurry mix it, the SKIM the top gunk like you're making chicken stock. You should only have to create 1-2 whirlpools, and different coffee beans elicit different amounts of bubbly fuzz gunk
  7. Wait 5 mins AND POUR

I prefer to drink this with a little milk (mostly to cool it down beccause it's still freaking hot after 5 mins) and nothing else. Oh, sugar if I'm feeling like I want to that day
